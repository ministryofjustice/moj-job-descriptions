The Role

We’re recruiting for an Associate Quality Engineer here at [](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/working-at-moj-ds/) [Justice Digital](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/working-at-moj-ds/), to be part of our warm and collaborative LAA Digital team, supporting the work of the Legal Aid Agency.

This role aligns against Tester (Test Engineer) from the Government Digital and Data Framework

The work we do is incredibly important to some of the most vulnerable people in our society. LAA Digital seeks to provide swift access to legal aid for those who need it and ensure efficient payment to providers of those services.

As an Associate Quality Engineer, you will be at the awareness stage of quality engineering, learning fundamental skills and developing your knowledge of tools and techniques. This is an exciting opportunity to gain hands-on experience in test strategies, test analysis, and automation efforts, with support and guidance to help you grow in the role.

This role will challenge you, but it will also be rewarding and impactful - helping to shape critical digital services that affect real people.

The Community

You’ll be part of a supportive engineering community, collaborating closely with developers, product managers, user researchers, designers, business analysts, and delivery managers to ensure that our services meet high-quality standards.

We foster a collaborative, inclusive, and transparent workplace where continuous improvement is at the core of what we do. We encourage learning, knowledge-sharing, and developing best practices in quality engineering.

We subscribe to the manifesto for agile software development. We’re open and transparent. We believe in building quality into our products, systems and services and that quality is everyone’s responsibility.

To help picture your life at [](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/working-at-moj-ds/) [MoJ Justice Digital](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/working-at-moj-ds/) please take a look at our [](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/) [blog](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/) and our [](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ministry-of-justice-digital-strategy-2025/ministry-of-justice-digital-strategy-2025) [Digital and Technology strategy 2025](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ministry-of-justice-digital-strategy-2025/ministry-of-justice-digital-strategy-2025)

Key Responsibilities:

-   Develop an understanding of quality assurance strategies, test planning, and execution methodologies.
    

-   Work under supervision to apply test approaches, ensuring they align with project needs.
    

-   Assist in analysing user stories, prototypes, and system designs to identify areas for testing.
    

-   Learn how to assess risks associated with software development and support mitigation strategies.
    

-   Gain exposure to different testing techniques, including functional and non-functional testing.
    

-   Support in designing, executing, and documenting test cases, ensuring they align with requirements and user needs.
    

-   Assist in identifying and reporting defects, learning how to escalate and track their resolution.
    

-   Develop a foundational understanding of automation frameworks and testing tools.
    

-   Gain hands-on experience with CI/CD pipelines, test environments, and reporting mechanisms.
    

-   Participate in team meetings and agile ceremonies, contributing insights from a quality perspective.
    

-   Learn how to collaborate with cross-functional teams to ensure testing processes integrate seamlessly.
    

-   Maintain a focus on continuous learning, keeping up to date with emerging trends in quality engineering.
    

If you are looking to kick-start a career in quality engineering, this role provides the perfect learning environment, with ample support and opportunities for growth.

If this feels like an exciting challenge, something you are enthusiastic about, and want to join our team please read on and apply!

Person Specification

Essential

-   Ability to understand and describe quality assurance principles.
    

-   Basic knowledge of testing methodologies, test case design and execution.
    

-   Awareness of functional and non-functional testing techniques.
    

-   Capability to critically analyse and inspect software artefacts for potential quality risks.
    

-   Understanding of how to track test progress and report on outcomes.
    

-   Strong communication and collaboration skills to engage with teams and stakeholders.
    

-   Willing to learn testing tools such as Playwright, Postman, Pact, or Cypress.
    

-   Knowledge of modern testing principles
    

-   Awareness of Agile methodologies, holistic testing practices and their impact on testing.
    

-   Know about CI/CD pipelines and DevOps principles.
