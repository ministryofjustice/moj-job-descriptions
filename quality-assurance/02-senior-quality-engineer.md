The Role

We’re recruiting for a Senior Quality Engineer here at [](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/working-at-moj-ds/) [Justice Digital](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/working-at-moj-ds/), to be part of our warm and collaborative LAA Digital team, supporting the work of the Legal Aid Agency.

This role aligns against [Senior Test Engineer](https://ddat-capability-framework.service.gov.uk/role/test-engineer#senior-test-engineer)  from the Government Digital and Data Framework

The work we do is incredibly important to some of the most vulnerable people in our society. LAA Digital seeks to provide swift access to legal aid for those who need it and ensure efficient payment to providers of those services.

As a Senior Quality Engineer, you will play a pivotal role in ensuring that digital services are reliable, scalable, and of high quality. Working alongside a multidisciplinary team, you will adopt a strategic approach to quality, ensuring test strategies and plans mitigate risk to the business goals. You will design and execute functional and non-functional tests, design and implement test automation frameworks to assist with testing and enable quality across multiple teams.

This is a hands-on role where you will have the opportunity to apply best practices, develop test automation, and integrate quality at every stage of development.

The community

You will be part of a collaborative and supportive engineering community, working with developers, product managers, user researchers, designers, business analysts, and delivery managers to embed quality into digital solutions.

We champion continuous learning, open communication, and agile methodologies, ensuring our teams thrive in an environment that is both inclusive and transparent.

We subscribe to the manifesto for agile software development. We’re open and transparent. We believe in building quality into our products, systems and services and that quality is everyone’s responsibility.

To help picture your life at [](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/working-at-moj-ds/) [MoJ Justice Digital](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/working-at-moj-ds/) please take a look at our [](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/) [blog](https://mojdigital.blog.gov.uk/) and our [](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ministry-of-justice-digital-strategy-2025/ministry-of-justice-digital-strategy-2025) [Digital and Technology strategy 2025](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ministry-of-justice-digital-strategy-2025/ministry-of-justice-digital-strategy-2025)

Key Responsibilities:

-   Work in multidisciplinary teams to deliver high-impact digital services for the Legal Aid Agency.
    

-   Provide technical and strategic leadership for multiple product teams, ensuring alignment with service outcomes and business goals.
    

-   Design and develop risk-based test strategies and plans that integrate testing throughout the entire software development lifecycle.
    

-   Advance automation initiatives, extending and standardising automated testing solutions using appropriate tools and frameworks.
    

-   Enable whole-team involvement in quality assurance activities at every phase of delivery.
    

-   Implement shift-left testing practices, ensuring early defect detection and prevention.
    

-   Contribute to the wider Justice Digital engineering community, sharing knowledge, mentoring junior engineers, and promoting best practices.
    

-   Continuously evolve and optimise test strategies, processes, and tooling.
    

-   Foster effective communication around quality and testing within and beyond your teams.
    

-   People manage and develop other Quality Engineers, supporting their growth and career progression.
    

If this feels like an exciting challenge, something you are enthusiastic about, and want to join our team please read on and apply!

How to Apply

Candidates must submit a CV and Coer Letter (750 words), which describes how you meet the requirements set out in the Person Specification above.

In Justice Digital, we recruit using a combination of the [](https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/digital-data-and-technology-profession-capability-framework) [Government Digital and Data Profession Capability](https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/digital-data-and-technology-profession-capability-framework)  and [](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/success-profiles) [Success Profiles](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/success-profiles) Frameworks. We will assess your Experience, Technical Skills and the following Behaviours during the assessment process:

-   Working Together
    

-   Managing a quality service
    

-   Communicating and influencing
    

-   Developing self and others
    

-   Changing and improving
    

A diverse panel will review your application against the Person Specification above.

Successful candidates who meet the required standard will then be invited to a 1.5 hour panel interview, which may include a test/task, held via video conference.

Should we receive a high volume of applications, a pre-sift based on:

-   Overseeing testing across multiple teams and throughout the entire software development lifecycle, demonstrating both technical and test leadership.
    

-   Design, build, and maintain automated test frameworks for UI and API testing using tools such as Postman, Selenium, Cypress, or Playwright.
    

-   A collaborative mindset, mentoring, coaching, growing and inspiring others to adopt holistic testing practices.
    

will be conducted before the sift.

The panel will be conducting a sift on following criteria from the Person Specification above in addition to the pre-sift criteria:

-   Practical experience with non-functional testing (Performance, Security, Accessibility) and related tooling.
    

-   Strong understanding of shift-left principles, including CI/CD pipelines.
    

-   Practical experience of designing and implementing various test types to deliver comprehensive test coverage.
    

-   Applied automation and testing best practices, improving frameworks and delivery pipelines.
    

Candidates who do not demonstrate examples/details of their experience of the requirements stated above in their Cover Letter will be rejected on this basis.

Should you be unsuccessful in the role that you have applied for but demonstrate the capability for a role at a lower level, we reserve the right to discuss this opportunity with you and offer you the position without needing a further application.

A reserve list may be held for up to 12 months, from which further appointments may be made.
